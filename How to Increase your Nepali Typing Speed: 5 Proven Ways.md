## 1. Familiarize: Explore the Visual Keyboard
Before typing fast, you need to know where the keys are. The Devanagari script layout can look complex, but nativetype makes it visual.

Homepage and Keyboard Page have an [Interactive Nepali Virtual Keyboard](https://nativetype.com/keyboard/nepali) where you can use keyboard or mouse to type.

Spend at least 5 mins just exploring. Don't type, just notice. This builds your mental map.

Print out a layout chart or keep it open on your screen. Your exercise is to mentally map the keys.


## 2. Build Muscle Memory: Follow "Learn Typing"
To build a muscle memory, you need structured, repetitive practice.

During this phase don't prioritize on speed.

The "Learn Typing" section offers [step-by-step typing lessons](https://nativetype.com/typing/nepali) to build speed and accuracy.

Start with Lesson 1. The platform will guide you through home row keys and simple combinations.

**Crucially, do not look at your physical keyboard.** Rely on the visual keyboard on screen and your fingers.

Repeat each lesson until you feel comfortable before moving on. Consistency here (15 minutes daily) builds the muscle memory you need.

## 3. Benchmark Your Progress: Take the Typing Test
You can't improve what you don't measure. Once you've built some muscle memory, it's time to see where you stand.

The [Nepali Typing Test](https://nativetype.com/test/nepali) feature with three distinct difficulty levels: Easy, Medium, and Hard.

Start with Easy. Once you're comfortable, switch to Medium and finally move to Hard level for complex vocabulary.

**Aim for 90%+ accuracy on each level before moving on**

## 4. Build Real Fluency: Use "Nativetype Document Editors"
Move from exercises to real-world flow and having fun while doing it.

## 5. Repeat Until You Hit Your Goal
Speed doesn't come from practicing once. Repetitive exercise builds muscle memory so you can move your fingers without even thinking.

Here is how you should move:
>**Test → Identify weaknesses → Drill → Test Again.**
